Lava/Magma Types

(There are three basic types of magma/lava: basaltic, andesitic, and rhyolitic, each of which has a different mineral composition. All types of magma have a significant percentage of silicon dioxide.)

Basaltic:

More fluid than andesites, which contain more silica.


Andesitic:

Commonly erupts from stratovolcanoes as thick lava flows, some reaching several km in length!


Rhyolitic:

Viscous and tend to form thick blocky lava flows or steep-sided piles of lava called lava domes
