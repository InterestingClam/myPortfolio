The Flows of Lava

There are 4 different types of flow.

Pahoehoe lava flow, Aa lava flow, Blocky lava flow, and also Pillow lava flow.

Pahoehoe lava is more like foam, and the pahoehoe that forms when the lava cools is very "shelly"; so-called because walking on it is like walking on large egg shells.

'A'ā (pronounced "ah-ah") is a Hawaiian term for lava flows that have a rough rubbly surface composed of broken lava blocks called clinkers.

Block lava flows have surfaces that consist of large angular blocks of lava. Blocky lava flows usually have andesitic or basaltic-andesitic compositions and are more viscous than basaltic lava flows that form pāhoehoe or 'a'ā. The blocks in block lavas are smoother than the rough clinkery ones in 'a'ā.

Pillow flows are produced by the piling up of individual pillow lava lobes. As a pillow flow forms, the newest pillows are erupted from the top of the stack and flow outward a limited distance before freezing, a process which tends to produce steep-sided mounds or ridges which can grow to be 10's of meters thick.
