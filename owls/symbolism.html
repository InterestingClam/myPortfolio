Owl Assosiated Symbolisms

In folklore they were seen as wise, helpful creatures with prophectal powers. While in medieval europe they were assosiated with witches, lonely and profane places. Owls have been seen as symbols of wisdom through out history, such as Minerva and her pet owl. Aswell as buddhism where they are messagers of trueth.

The greek godess Athena's representation and pet was an owl. Many tribes across the country not only believe that owls have a correlation to death, but also the afterlife. Tribes such as the Lakota, Omaha, Cheyenne, Fox, Ojibwa, Menominee, Cherokee, and Creek consider owls to be either an embodied spirit of the dead or associated with a spirit in some way. In the Middle East, the owl is linked with destruction, ruin and death. They are believed to represent the souls of people who have died un-avenged. Seeing an owl on the way to battle foretells a bloody battle with many deaths and casualties. Seeing an owl at somebody's house predicts their death.
